Section D: Accountability

Accountability in AI chatbot development refers to the obligation of developers, organizations, and the chatbot itself to take responsibility for its actions, outcomes, and impacts. This includes ensuring that the chatbot adheres to ethical standards, operates transparently, and addresses any harm or issues that arise from its use.

D6 Transparency in Limitations

Definition:

The chatbot should have the ability to communicate its limitations clearly to users.

Techniques:

  • Limitation Statements: ensure the chatbot proactively informs users about its limitations, such as areas where it might not provide accurate information.
  • Capability Disclosures: make the chatbot disclose when it is operating beyond its training or knowledge base.
  • User Guides: provide users with guidelines on what the chatbot can and cannot do, ensuring realistic expectations.

Section E: Transparency

Transparency in AI chatbots involve being open and clear about the chatbot’s operation, the data is uses, and the decisions it makes. This section lists the specific goals that need to be achieved to ensure the users understand the chatbot’s functions, processes, and limitations, to better foster trust in the chatbot. These goals include explainability, data usage transparency, algorithmic transparency, capability disclosure, and public documentation.

E3 Algorithmic Transparency

Definition:

The chatbot’s algorithms and decision-making processes should be accessible and understandable to users.

Techniques:

  • Algorithm Summaries: provide summaries or explanations of the algorithms and models underlying the chatbot.
  • Transparent Model Training: showcase the data sources and model training processes of the chatbots.
  • Error Disclosure: ensure the chatbot openly acknowledges when it makes an error and allow users to see the error correction mechanisms.

Section D: Accountability

Accountability in AI chatbot development refers to the obligation of the developers and the chatbot to accept their responsibilities and face consequences whenever necessary. This section lists the specific goals that need to be achieved to ensure the accountability principles are upheld to a reasonable standard. These goals include clear role assignments, self-monitoring, corrective actions, incident response, and regulatory compliance.

D4 Incident Response

Definition:

Developers should draft procedures to handle incidents where the chatbot causes harm, makes errors, or operates outside its intended scope.

Techniques:

  • Incident Reporting System: set up a system for users to report issues related to the chatbot.
  • Redress Mechanisms: provide clear pathways for users to seek redress or compensation when harmed by the chatbotâ€™s actions.
  • Post-Incident Analysis: conduct thorough investigations of incidents to determine causes and prevent recurrence.

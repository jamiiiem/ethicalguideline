Section D: Accountability

Accountability in AI chatbot development refers to the obligation of developers, organizations, and the chatbot itself to take responsibility for its actions, outcomes, and impacts. This includes ensuring that the chatbot adheres to ethical standards, operates transparently, and addresses any harm or issues that arise from its use.

D12 Stakeholder Engagement

Definition:

Developers should involve stakeholders, including users, developers, and external parties, in the accountability process to ensure diverse perspectives and comprehensive oversight.

Techniques:

  • Stakeholder Panels: create panels that include stakeholders from various backgrounds to review and provide input on the chatbot’s development and performance.
  • Public Consultations: conduct public consultations to gather feedback and insights from the broader community on the chatbot’s impact and accountability measures.

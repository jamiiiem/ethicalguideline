Section G: Inclusiveness and Fairness

Inclusiveness and Fairness in an educational AI chatbot involve catering to diverse demographics and actively mitigating biases. This section outlines the specific goals that need to be achieved to best promote inclusiveness and fairness. These goals include bias mitigation, fair decision-making, accessibility and customisation, and diversity.

G3 Accessibility and Customisation

Definition:

The chatbot should be accessible to all groups of users, including those with disabilities. Users should be allowed to control and customise their experience with the chatbot.

Techniques:

  • Accessible Design: incorporate accessible design principles whenever applicable.
  • Multimodal Interaction: enable multimodal interaction, including text, voice, and images, to accommodate different preferences and needs.
  • Compliance with Accessibility Standards: reference and adhere to established accessibility standards.
  • Customisation Options: provide users with options to customise the settings and opt out of certain functionalities.

Examples:

  • Accessible design principles may include supporting screen readers, providing alt-text for images, and using high contrast colours.
  • Customisation options may include options to choose the chatbot’s tone, language, and interaction style to better meet the users’ preferences and needs. Users should also be allowed to opt out of data collection or decision-making processes that may lead to unfair treatment.

Section A: Privacy and Data Protection

AI chatbots that deal regularly with educational content should handle them and interact with users with the highest standards of privacy and data security. This section lists the specific goals that need to be achieved in order to ensure that the privacy and data protection is upheld to a reasonable standard. These goals include data anonymisation, data minimisation, informed consent, secure data storage and transmission, user control, compliance with laws, as well as privacy audits.

A3 Informed Consent

Definition:

Data should only be collected after obtaining the user’s consent. The communication should be transparent, and the consent should be clearly given.

Techniques:

  • Simplify Privacy Policies: use clear and straightforward language to ensure the users can fully comprehend the content.
  • Active Consent: require an active, confirmed consent from the users before collecting data.
  • Parental Consent for Minors: obtain parental consent when dealing with data collected from users who are minors.

Examples:

A screen with the privacy policy may pop up at the landing page of the chatbot. A checkbox that asks for the users’ consent may be present, asking users to select the corresponding checkboxes before proceeding with data collection.

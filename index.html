Data Anonymisation

Definition:

Data should be collected with efforts to encrypt or erase user’s private, sensitive, and identifiable information.

Techniques:

  • Data Masking: Substitute identifiable data with randomised data using data shuffling or data manipulation ideas.
  • Tokenisation: Convert sensitive data into equivalent tokens that can be used in the system without exposing the original data.
  • Noise Addition: Introduce minor randomisation to the data to ensure that individual identities cannot be traced back.
  • Data Generalisation: The precision of sensitive information can be reduced, generalising it by categorising data into groups.

Considerations:

Developers should aim to strike a balance between privacy and utility of data. Data protection measures are necessary and important, but overusing protection mechanisms may also lead to a lack of relevance and precision.

Examples:

The actual names of users can be converted into randomised strings to avoid storing sensitive training data. Plus, instead of using their precise age, data generalisation can be performed to categorise them into age groups of, for example, 15 to 18 years old, or 18-21 years old.
